Why You Need to Resize Image Without Losing Quality

High-resolution images are great for printing, but they are a nightmare for website performance. Large files slow down your page load speed, which impacts your SEO rankings and user experience. By using our tool to reduce image size online, you ensure that your site stays fast and responsive.

How Image Compression Works

Compression reduces the bits used to represent an image. Lossy compression (like JPG) removes invisible data to drastically reduce size, while lossless compression (like PNG) keeps every pixel intact. Our image compressor online allows you to find the perfect balance between size and quality.

The Benefits of Browser-Based Image Editing

Safety first! When you use traditional online editors, your photos are uploaded to a remote server. PixelResize is different. We use client-side JavaScript to process images. This means your data never leaves your browser, protecting your privacy and saving you bandwidth.

Bulk Image Resizer for Professionals

Got hundreds of photos? No problem. Our bulk image resizer allows you to process multiple files simultaneously. Set your dimensions once, and let your browser do the heavy lifting in seconds.

JPG vs PNG vs WebP: Which Format Should You Use?

  • JPG: Best for photographs and complex colors. Great compression but lossy.
  • PNG: Best for logos and graphics with transparency. Lossless but larger files.
  • WebP: The modern web choice. Provides superior compression for both lossy and lossless formats.
